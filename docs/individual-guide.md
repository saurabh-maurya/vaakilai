# VakilAI — Individual User Guide

**For:** Citizens, individuals, business owners, and anyone seeking legal help in India
**Platform:** VakilAI Consumer Portal (vakilai.in)
**Last updated:** June 2026

---

## What is VakilAI?

VakilAI is an AI-powered legal platform designed to make Indian law accessible to every citizen. Whether you need to understand your rights, generate a legal document, track a court case, or find a qualified lawyer — VakilAI is your one-stop legal assistant.

---

## Getting Started

### Create Your Account
1. Visit **vakilai.in** and click **Get Started Free**
2. Enter your name, email, and mobile number
3. Verify your mobile via OTP
4. Choose **Individual / Business** when asked for your role

**Free Plan:** 5 AI queries per day | Basic document generation | 3 tracked cases

## Features Available to You

---

### 1. AI Legal Chat (`/chat`)

**What it does:** Ask any legal question in plain English or Hindi. Our AI provides jurisdiction-aware answers with source citations.

**How to use:**
1. Click **AI Legal Chat** in the left sidebar
2. Type your question — e.g. *"Can my landlord evict me without notice in Delhi?"*
3. The AI responds with:
   - A clear answer in plain language
   - Relevant laws and sections cited
   - Confidence score (High/Medium/Low)
   - Suggestion to consult a lawyer if needed
4. Continue the conversation to ask follow-up questions

**Best practices:**
- Provide jurisdiction (state/city) for more accurate answers
- Include relevant facts: dates, amounts, parties involved
- Use the AI to understand concepts, then consult a lawyer for your specific case

**Topics covered:** Property law, family law, consumer rights, employment, contracts, criminal, banking, taxation, intellectual property, and 20+ more areas.

---

### 2. Document Generation (`/documents`)

**What it does:** Generate 150+ types of legal documents — rental agreements, affidavits, legal notices, contracts — tailored to Indian law.

**How to use:**
1. Click **Documents** → **Generate New**
2. Search for your document type (e.g. "rent agreement")
3. Answer the guided questionnaire (parties, dates, amounts, terms)
4. Preview the AI-generated document
5. Download as PDF or Word
6. Optionally, request AI review to flag risky clauses

**Available document types include:**
- Rental/Lease Agreements
- Legal Notices (Consumer, Cheque Bounce, Eviction)
- Affidavits (General, Address Proof, Income)
- Non-Disclosure Agreements
- Employment Agreements
- Sale Deeds (draft)
- Partnership Agreements
- Power of Attorney
- Demand Letters
- Complaint letters

**Note:** Generated documents are starting drafts. Have a lawyer review them before use in court proceedings.

### 3. Know Your Rights (`/rights`)

**What it does:** Learn about your fundamental and legal rights in plain language, organized by life situation.

**How to use:**
1. Click **Know Your Rights** in the sidebar
2. Browse categories: Tenant Rights, Employee Rights, Consumer Rights, Arrested Person Rights, Woman & Child Rights, Borrower Rights, and more
3. Click any category to get a detailed breakdown with the applicable laws
4. Use the search bar to find rights for a specific situation

**Topics covered:**
- Rights when arrested or detained
- Rights as a tenant
- Rights against employer
- Consumer rights and remedies
- Right to information (RTI)
- Women's legal protections
- Rights of persons with disabilities

---

### 4. Find a Lawyer (`/lawyers`)

**What it does:** Browse, filter, and book consultations with verified Indian lawyers.

**How to use:**
1. Click **Find a Lawyer**
2. Filter by:
   - **Practice area** (Family, Criminal, Property, etc.)
   - **City / Court**
   - **Language**
   - **Fee range**
   - **Availability**
3. View lawyer profile: experience, Bar Council number, reviews, past cases
4. Click **Book Consultation** to schedule
5. Pay consultation fee via Razorpay (UPI, cards, netbanking)
6. Attend via video call or in-person

**AI Matching:** Click **Find Best Match** — describe your problem and our AI will shortlist the top 3 lawyers for your specific issue.

---

### 5. eCourts Tracker (`/ecourts`)

**What it does:** Track the status of any court case across district courts, high courts, and Supreme Court. Get hearing date alerts.

**How to use:**
1. Click **eCourts Tracker**
2. Click **Track New Case**
3. Enter:
   - Case number (e.g. *WP 1234/2024*)
   - Court type: District / High Court / Supreme Court
   - State and district
4. The system fetches live status from eCourts API
5. If not found, enter details manually
6. View next hearing date, case status, last orders
7. Click **View Cause List** to see tomorrow's listed cases for any court

**Alerts:** Enable notifications to receive SMS/email reminders before hearing dates.

---

### 6. IPC → BNS Converter (`/ipc-bns`)

**What it does:** Convert old Indian Penal Code sections to new Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita 2023 sections (effective 1 July 2024).

**How to use:**
1. Click **IPC → BNS** in the sidebar
2. Type a section number (e.g. *420*) or keyword (e.g. *cheating*, *murder*)
3. See the corresponding BNS section, title, and any notes on changes
4. Use the reverse search to convert BNS back to IPC

**Why it matters:** All new FIRs, charge sheets, and pleadings filed after 1 July 2024 must use BNS sections. Old IPC-based documents may need updating.

---

### 7. Legal News (`/news`)

**What it does:** Stay updated with the latest legal developments, Supreme Court judgments, new legislation, and regulatory updates.

**How to use:**
1. Click **Legal News** in the sidebar
2. Browse by category: General, Constitutional, Corporate, Criminal, Consumer, Technology, Legislation
3. Search for specific topics or cases
4. Click any article to read on the source website (Bar & Bench / LiveLaw)
5. Refresh manually for latest updates (cached every 15 minutes)

---

### 8. Compliance (`/compliance`)

**What it does:** Check compliance requirements for your business or personal situation — GST, FSSAI, Shops & Establishments, labour laws, and more.

**How to use:**
1. Click **Compliance**
2. Select your business type and state
3. Get a checklist of applicable registrations, filings, and deadlines
4. Track which items are done, pending, or overdue

---

### 9. Lok Adalat / ODR (`/odr`)

**What it does:** Explore alternative dispute resolution options — Lok Adalat, Online Dispute Resolution (ODR), and mediation.

**How to use:**
1. Click **Lok Adalat / ODR**
2. Describe your dispute
3. See if it qualifies for Lok Adalat (motor accident, cheque bounce, pre-litigation matters)
4. Find empanelled ODR service providers
5. Initiate an ODR case online (no court needed)

**Advantage:** Lok Adalat settlements are final and cannot be appealed — faster and cheaper than court.
